# Can Someone from Porto Alegre Sell Their PV Panel Energy?

**Context**: Porto Alegre, Brazil (BR POA) — Pre-feasibility scope for energy project preparation  
**Date**: March 19, 2026

---

**Not** in the simple “I own rooftop solar, so I invoice my tenant per kWh like a utility” sense.

In Brazil, there are two different regimes here. One is **distributed generation / net-billing compensation (SCEE)**, where surplus energy becomes **credits** that offset consumption. ANEEL lists the allowed SCEE models as: local self-consumption, **remote self-consumption only for units under the same holder**, multiple-consumer-unit developments, and shared generation through a consortium/cooperative/association. That same-holder rule is the main reason a landlord usually **cannot just take credits from their plant and apply them to a tenant's separate meter** when the tenant has a different CPF/CNPJ. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][1])

So the practical answer is:

---

## 1. If you mean “can I legally monetize solar for tenants today?” — yes, but usually through one of these structures:

### A. Lease / service-charge structure

Best for a single building today. You install PV on the property, use it to reduce the building's electricity cost, and recover part of that value through **rent, condomínio charges, or a fixed solar/service fee in the lease**. That is not the same as becoming an electricity retailer. This is the safer path when the tenant is in a normal leased space and you want a clean commercial model. This point is an inference from how Brazilian regulation separates SCEE compensation from formal electricity commercialization. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][1])

### B. Multiple-consumer-unit project (condominium / multi-unit development)

If the building is structured as an **empreendimento de múltiplas unidades consumidoras**, ANEEL allows the generated energy to be **allocated among the units** in percentages or priority order defined by the participants. That is the cleanest regulated model for some mixed-use / condo-style assets. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][1])

### C. Shared generation (geração compartilhada)

If the tenants are separate companies or individuals with separate utility accounts, the more natural regulated path is often **shared generation**: consortium, cooperative, condominium or other civil association formed for that purpose, with the plant's output used to compensate the participants' bills. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][1])

---

## 2. If you mean “can I literally sell electricity as a supplier business?” — only inside the electricity commercialization framework.

Brazil's commercialization rules are sector-regulated. Federal law frames electricity commercialization among concessionaires, permissionaries, authorized agents, and the eligible consumers in the free market, not as a general unlicensed resale activity for any landlord. ([Planalto][2])

That means **true retail supply to a tenant** points you toward the **free market (ACL)**, not ordinary leasing.

- **High-voltage tenants (Grupo A):** already eligible. Since January 2024, all high-voltage consumers can participate in the free market, generally represented by a **commercializador varejista**. ANEEL also states that consumers below 500 kW in this opening are represented before the CCEE by a retail marketer. ([CCEE][3])
- **Low-voltage commercial/industrial tenants:** the 2025 reform opened supplier choice **from 1 August 2026**. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][4])
- **Low-voltage residential tenants:** supplier choice opens **from 1 December 2027**. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][4])

So, as of **March 19, 2026**:

| Tenant type | Can sell as supplier? |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Residential tenant** | **No**, not as a normal electricity supplier yet. Use lease/condo/shared-generation structures instead. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][4]) |
| **Commercial/industrial tenant in low voltage** | **Not yet**, unless you wait for the low-voltage opening from **August 1, 2026** or use a non-retail structure now. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][4]) |
| **Commercial/industrial tenant in high voltage** | **Yes**, via the ACL/free-market route with the proper market-agent setup, not just by owning the panels. ([CCEE][3]) |

---

## Summary: Practical routes for a building-owner business model in 2026

1. **Bundle the solar benefit into rent / service charges**, or
2. **Set up a shared-generation / multi-unit structure**, or
3. For larger eligible tenants, **move them into the ACL with a licensed retail marketer/commercializer setup**. ([Serviços e Informações do Brasil][1])
