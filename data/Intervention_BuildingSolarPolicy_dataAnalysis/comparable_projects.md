# Comparable Projects — Building Solar Mandate Policy

**Intervention:** Solar Mandate Policies for Residential and Commercial Buildings (POA-E-06)
**Filter criteria:** City-level policies that mandate or incentivize distributed solar PV on private residential/commercial buildings through building codes, fiscal incentives, or certification programs. Excludes public-buildings-only programs and utility-scale solar.

---

## 1. Seoul — Solar City Seoul (South Korea)

- **What:** City-wide program to install solar on 1 million homes by 2022, including mini-solar PVs on 540,000 apartment balconies, 90,000 rental homes, and 370,000 buildings. Combines subsidies, BIPV incentives, and building retrofit requirements.
- **Policy instruments:** Municipal subsidy for small solar (<50 kW); BIPV incentive scheme (KRW 4–6M/kW); Building Retrofit Programme (BRP) bundling solar with efficiency.
- **Scale:** 1 GW rooftop solar target; 160,000+ homes with solar by 2022; South Korea surpassed 1.4 GW residential solar by end of 2022.
- **Relevance:** Closest match to Porto Alegre's combined mandate + incentive + certification approach. Apartment balcony mini-solar directly applicable to POA's dense residential stock. Subsidy design informed by income-based targeting.
- **Sources:**
  - EBRD Green Cities: https://www.ebrdgreencities.com/policy-tool/solar-city-seoul-seoul-south-korea-2/
  - pv magazine (1 GW plan): https://www.pv-magazine.com/2019/11/18/seoul-launches-1-gw-rooftop-solar-plan/
  - pv magazine (BIPV scheme): https://www.pv-magazine.com/2022/05/02/seoul-launches-bipv-incentive-scheme/
  - Metropolis (One Less Nuclear Power Plant): https://use.metropolis.org/case-studies/one-less-nuclear-power-plant
  - Spatial diffusion study (PMC): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7828634/

## 2. San Francisco — Solar Mandate for New Construction (USA)

- **What:** First US city to mandate solar PV or solar thermal on all new buildings (residential and commercial) up to 10 stories, effective January 2017. Embedded in building code via local amendment to California's Title 24.
- **Policy instruments:** Building code mandate (not incentive); applies to all new construction; choice between solar PV, solar thermal, or living roof.
- **Scale:** Applies to all new buildings ≤10 stories in a city of 870,000.
- **Relevance:** Direct precedent for POA's Sub-action 6.4 (building code update). Demonstrates how a city can mandate solar on private new construction. San Francisco later expanded to require all-electric new construction.
- **Sources:**
  - C40 Renewable Energy Declaration reference: https://www.c40.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/2947_EN_-_Renewable_Energy_Declaration_Text.original.pdf
  - SF Environment (original ordinance): San Francisco Environment Code Chapter 99, Ordinance No. 221-16

## 3. Delhi — Solar Energy Policy for Rooftop PV (India)

- **What:** City-level solar policy targeting 2 GW rooftop solar by 2025 across residential and commercial buildings. Combines generation-based incentives, net metering, and capital subsidies for residential systems.
- **Policy instruments:** Capital subsidy for residential (up to 40% for first 3 kW); net metering; mandatory solar on government buildings as demonstration; commercial incentive via accelerated depreciation.
- **Scale:** Target 2 GW by 2025 (of which 40% rooftop) in a city of 20M+.
- **Relevance:** Large developing-country city using tiered incentives by building segment (residential gets subsidy, commercial gets depreciation). Similar economic context to POA (high residential tariff driving payback).
- **Sources:**
  - C40 case study: https://www.c40.org/case-studies/solar-policy-delhi-2016/

## 4. Curitiba — Rooftop Solar on Municipal + Social Housing (Brazil)

- **What:** C40 Cities Finance Facility-supported program solarizing municipal rooftops and piloting rooftop PV on 26 social housing units. Includes bus terminals and City Hall.
- **Policy instruments:** Municipal investment + C40 CFF technical assistance; pilot on social housing as proof-of-concept for scaling to private sector.
- **Scale:** Pilot phase; 1,150 tCO2/year reduction forecast.
- **Relevance:** **Same country (Brazil), same regulatory framework (MMGD net metering), same grid operator type.** Social housing pilot directly relevant to POA's equity-weighted IPTU Sustentável design. CFF engagement model is a precedent for POA.
- **Sources:**
  - C40 CFF project page: https://c40cff.org/projects/curitiba-solar-energy
  - Metropolis case study: https://use.metropolis.org/case-studies/curitiba-more-energy

## 5. Guangzhou — Solar PV Subsidy Program (China)

- **What:** Municipal regulations providing subsidies (0.2 yuan/W installed or 0.15 yuan/kWh generated) for industrial, commercial, and residential rooftop solar. 162 projects funded via municipal special fund between 2014–2017.
- **Policy instruments:** Per-watt or per-kWh municipal subsidy; encouragement (not mandate) for all building types to use rooftops for solar.
- **Scale:** 220 MW installed capacity by mid-2017; 37.83M yuan municipal fund disbursed to 162 projects.
- **Relevance:** Subsidy-driven (not mandate-driven) approach for private buildings. Per-kWh incentive model is an alternative to POA's property-tax-based IPTU Sustentável. Tracked per-project results useful for M&V design.
- **Sources:**
  - C40 case study: https://www.c40.org/case-studies/implementation-of-solar-pv-in-guangzhou/

## 6. Chennai — Solar Rooftop Mandate for Government + Commercial Buildings (India)

- **What:** Greater Chennai Corporation mandated solar rooftops on all 1,378 GCC buildings, with policy extending to all new government and local body buildings. Commercial building uptake driven by state-level mandate.
- **Policy instruments:** Mandatory installation on government buildings; phased rollout; Smart City funding.
- **Scale:** 1,378 government buildings targeted; city of 7M+.
- **Relevance:** Government-led mandate that creates demonstration effect for private sector. Phased rollout approach relevant to POA's timeline (8% by 2030 → 40% by 2050).
- **Sources:**
  - C40 case study: https://www.c40.org/case-studies/installation-of-solar-rooftops-in-greater-chennai-s-corporate-buildings/

## 7. Durban (eThekwini) — Solar Framework for Citizens (South Africa)

- **What:** Municipal solar framework enabling citizens and businesses to install rooftop PV and feed excess into the grid. Pilot of 300 kWp on 5 municipal rooftops, with city-wide scaling plan.
- **Policy instruments:** Municipal framework for grid-connected distributed generation; net billing; estimated $10M annual savings if scaled to all municipal buildings.
- **Scale:** Pilot: 300 kWp, 82 tCO2 reduced in 2 months. Full scale: all municipal buildings.
- **Relevance:** Developing-country city creating regulatory framework for distributed solar. Similar challenge to POA: working within national utility framework (Eskom / CEEE Equatorial) to enable private rooftop solar.
- **Sources:**
  - C40 case study (EOS project): https://www.c40.org/case-studies/durban-s-energy-office-solar-eos-project/
  - C40 case study (citizen framework): https://www.c40.org/case-studies/cities100-durban-solar-framework-calls-citizens-to-action/

## 8. Bogotá — Energy Efficiency + Renewable Energy on Buildings (Colombia)

- **What:** C40 CFF-supported project developing concepts for energy efficiency and rooftop PV on public and selected private buildings, as part of Bogotá's Climate Action Plan (50% GHG reduction by 2030, carbon neutrality by 2050).
- **Policy instruments:** C40 CFF technical assistance for project structuring; building prioritization methodology for PV + efficiency.
- **Scale:** Part of citywide climate action plan; building-by-building prioritization.
- **Relevance:** **Latin American city, similar institutional context.** C40 CFF building prioritization methodology directly applicable to POA. Climate Action Plan integration mirrors PLAC structure.
- **Sources:**
  - C40 CFF project page: https://c40cff.org/projects/energy-efficiency-and-renewable-energy-on-public-buildings
