### What It Does

Scale distributed solar PV in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings through three coordinated mechanisms:

1. **Green certification** — Municipal Sustainable Certification Program (Decree 21.789/2022) rewards efficient lighting, renewable generation, and nature-based solutions integration
2. **Mandatory building code updates** — Update the Building Code with mandatory energy and water efficiency criteria, including a Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) tool
3. **Fiscal incentives** — Expand IPTU Sustentavel (property tax incentives) for new sustainable builds and retrofits

The government spends R$6M on program administration and incentive design. The private sector invests R$658M. This is a policy-leverage action, not a capital project.

### 4.2 Methodology

Private sector drives 99% of investment. Government cost covers only certification program administration and incentive design (R$6M). GHG impact is attributed to the government enabling role.

**Geospatial inputs used:**
- `br_ibge`: 456,770 households → 36,542 households at 8% 2030 target
- `global_solar_atlas`: 1,405 kWh/kWp/year PVOUT for Porto Alegre (3.85 kWh/kWp/day)
- `ghsl_built_up`: ~40% built-up fraction → rooftop availability assumption

### 4.3 Rating Rationale

> Extremely low government cost per tCO2e (R$9) due to massive private sector leverage. 4-year payback for private investors makes this self-sustaining. Largest absolute GHG impact among city-level actions (26,702 tCO2e/yr by 2030).

### 4.4 Confidence Note

Building PV adoption rate is uncertain; depends on incentive uptake. The 8% residential target by 2030 is the key assumption. Actual adoption could be higher (if tariffs rise) or lower (if financing is unavailable).

## 6. Nature-Based Solutions Integration

**NbS Relevance:** High

The certification program explicitly integrates NbS in building design:
- **Green roofs** — insulation (reduces heating/cooling 15-30%) + stormwater retention
- **Urban vegetation** — shade, natural ventilation, biodiversity
- **Solar + green roof hybrid** — co-located on the same building footprint for dual mitigation benefit per m²

### NbS Opportunity: Urban Solar + Green Roof Hybrid

> Install solar PV panels on green roofs of public buildings and dense residential blocks. Green roof provides insulation (reduces heating/cooling 15-30%), while PV generates electricity. Co-located = dual mitigation benefit per m².

**Prioritization datasets:**
- `global_solar_atlas`: Identify highest irradiance rooftops
- `ghsl_built_up`: Flat-roof building stock suitable for green roof + PV
- `modis_ndvi`: Low NDVI = urban heat island priority zones
- `ghsl_population`: Dense residential areas for maximum impact

**Co-benefits:** thermal comfort, biodiversity, stormwater retention, air quality

## 8. Context Signals

### Supportive Signals (from energy catalog)

- High electricity tariffs (>0.12 USD/kWh) — drives private sector payback
- Old building stock (>20 years) — large retrofit opportunity
- No prior comprehensive efficiency program — first-mover advantage
- Municipal climate action plan commitment (PLAC) — political mandate
- Net metering policy active (Brazil MMGD) — enables distributed solar economics
- Solar GHI > 1,400 kWh/m²/year — strong solar resource in Porto Alegre

### Blocking Signals

- Buildings scheduled for demolition or major renovation — limits near-term pool
- Building PV adoption rate uncertain — depends on incentive uptake (confidence: Medium)

### Regional Context

- Porto Alegre is one of RS's largest energy demand centers but produces little electricity
- The city already purchases 100% renewable energy for ~60 public facilities
- Stationary energy accounts for ~23% of city GHG emissions
- RS grid is already 87.5% renewable — building solar displaces a relatively clean grid, which limits tCO2e per MWp but still contributes to energy independence and resilience

## 11. Gaps and Opportunities

### Gaps vs. C40 Framework

| Gap | C40 HIA | Current PLAC Position | Opportunity |
|-----|---------|----------------------|-------------|
| No mandatory net-zero pathway for new buildings | HIA 10 | Code update proposed (6.4) but not net-zero trajectory | Strengthen sub-action 6.4 to include a mandatory net-zero pathway by 2040/2050 |
| No mandatory standards for existing buildings | HIA 11 | Existing buildings covered by incentives only | Add mandatory retrofit requirements for large commercial/industrial buildings (>500 m²) |
| Embodied carbon not primary focus | HIA 17 | LCA tool mentioned in 6.4 | Expand LCA tool scope to require embodied carbon reporting in new construction |

### Opportunities

1. **Pair with POA-E-07** (public buildings solar) — government buildings demonstrate what the private sector is incentivized to do. Sequence POA-E-07 in parallel to create visible public examples.
2. **Carbon market connection** — RS-E-04 (carbon market) could allow certified buildings to generate carbon credits, adding a revenue stream beyond energy savings.
3. **Equity targeting** — Use br_ibge census data to direct IPTU Sustentavel incentives toward low-income neighborhoods, ensuring the green building transition doesn't only benefit wealthy areas.
4. **Post-flood resilience** — After May 2024 floods, building code updates can integrate flood resilience standards alongside energy efficiency, addressing both adaptation and mitigation.
