# Top Actions for Porto Alegre Energy Project Preparation

**City:** Porto Alegre (BR POA)
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Basis:** Overlap analysis of C40/HIAP High Impact Actions and Brazil local plans (PLAC POA + RS ProClima)

---

## Prioritized Action List

### Action 1: Solar PV on Public Buildings (37 MWp)

**What:** Install rooftop solar on 500 municipal schools (50 kWp each) and 60 public facilities (200 kWp each) via PPP model. City pays 30% of CAPEX.

**Cost-effectiveness:** R$296/tCO2e | 6,759 tCO2e/yr avoided | R$50M city share of R$166.5M total

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 15** — Policies towards net zero carbon municipal buildings | Direct match. C40 calls for net zero municipal buildings through retrofits and onsite renewables. POA targets 100% renewable public buildings by 2030. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 12** — Grid decarbonization (building-scale) | Solar on 560 public buildings contributes 37 MWp of distributed generation to local grid decarbonization. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-E-07** — Public Buildings Energy Transition Plan | Explicit PLAC action. PPP already operating for 60 facilities. 10 biodigesters already installed in schools. Lead: SMAMUS. |
| **PLAC** | **RS-E-03** — RS Renewable Energy Policy Package | State solar incentive laws (Lei 14.898/2016) provide the regulatory framework enabling this municipal action. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `solar-public-buildings` | Proven maturity, 6-18 month timeline. Financing: municipal-budget + climate-fund-grant + dfi-concessional. |

**Why #1:** 100% within municipal control. PPP model already tested. Highest implementation certainty among all actions. Pairs with building efficiency (Action 2) for compounding savings.

### Action 2: Sustainable Buildings — Private Sector Leverage (146 MWp enabled)

**What:** Scale distributed solar in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings through green certification (Decree 21.789/2022), mandatory Building Code updates, and fiscal incentives (IPTU Sustentavel). Government cost is R$6M for program administration; private sector invests R$658M.

**Cost-effectiveness:** R$9/tCO2e (govt) | 26,702 tCO2e/yr avoided | Largest absolute GHG impact among city actions

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 14** — Renewable energy mandates for buildings | Direct match. POA sets mandatory renewable targets: 8% residential (2030), 25% (2040), 40% (2050). |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 16** — Retrofit programs for private buildings | IPTU Sustentavel provides fiscal incentives for green retrofits in existing private buildings. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 10** — Building energy codes towards net zero | PLAC sub-action 6.4 proposes updating Building Code with mandatory energy/water efficiency (LCA tool). Partial alignment — code update proposed but not yet a mandatory net-zero pathway. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 11** — Mandatory energy performance standards | PLAC sub-action 6.2 proposes mandatory certification for new constructions. Existing buildings covered by incentives only. Partial alignment. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-E-06** — Foster sustainable construction and green technologies | Core PLAC energy action. 6 sub-actions covering certification, code reform, incentives, and monitoring. Lead: SMAMUS. Partners: SMP, SMF. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `building-efficiency-public` | Proven maturity, 6-12 month timeline for efficiency measures. |

**Why #2:** Best cost-effectiveness ratio (R$9/tCO2e) due to massive private sector leverage. R$1 of government spending enables R$110 of private investment. Aligns with 4 C40 HIAs simultaneously — the broadest alignment of any action.

### Action 3: Biogas from Organic Waste (200 school biodigesters)

**What:** Scale biodigester program from 10 to 200 school-scale units. Treats 36,500 t/yr of organic waste, generates 4,380 MWh/yr of biogas energy, and produces biofertilizer for school gardens.

**Cost-effectiveness:** R$43/tCO2e | 18,819 tCO2e/yr avoided (18,250 from waste + 569 from energy) | R$20M government CAPEX

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 39** — Organic waste treatment | Direct match. C40 calls for treating significant amounts of organic waste. POA biodigesters convert school food waste to energy + fertilizer. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 23** — Sanitary landfill (gas management) | Indirect. Regional landfills at Minas do Leao and Sao Leopoldo already produce ~100,000 m3/day biogas. School biodigesters are the distributed complement. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 13** — Phase out fossil fuel heating/cooking | Indirect. Biomethane replaces fossil gas in cooking. No explicit phase-out policy, but biomethane substitution is a step in that direction. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-E-07** sub-actions 7.2, 7.4, 7.5 | School composting/biodigester program explicitly listed. 10 units already installed. Lead: SMAMUS + SMED. |
| **PLAC** | **RS-E-02** — Biogas-RS Program | State program provides regulatory framework, supply chain support, and biomethane policy (Lei 14.864/2016). |
| **PLAC** | **Pacto do Biometano** | POA is an active participant. Provides market coordination for biomethane offtake. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `biomethane-waste` | Proven maturity, 18-36 month timeline. Financing: ppp-light + dfi-concessional + climate-fund-grant. |

**Why #3:** Unique double-benefit action — every biodigester avoids methane emissions from waste AND displaces fossil energy. Third-best cost-effectiveness. Already has 10 operational units proving the model works. Strong NbS nexus (food-energy-water cycle in schools).

### Action 4: Electric Bus Fleet (100+ buses)

**What:** Replace diesel buses with electric buses and deploy charging infrastructure. Initial commitment of 100 buses with pathway to scale across the fleet.

**Cost-effectiveness:** 40-80 tCO2e/bus/year avoided | Significant PM2.5 and NOx reduction along routes

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 1** — Procure only zero-emission buses | Direct match. POA committed to 100 e-buses. C40 calls for transit operators to procure only zero-emission buses. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 5** — Transport electrification | Direct match. E-bus fleet is the primary transport electrification entry point for POA. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 6** — Zero-emission municipal vehicles | Broader scope. PLS (POA-E-08) includes sustainable procurement criteria that extend to municipal fleet beyond buses. |
| **PLAC** | **PLAC commitment: 100 electric buses** | Explicit city commitment in the Climate Action Plan. Charging infrastructure being planned. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-E-08** sub-actions | Sustainable Logistics Plan includes fleet procurement reform as part of behavioral change across public administration. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `electric-bus-fleet` | Scaling maturity tier, 12-36 month timeline. Financing: dfi-concessional + climate-fund-grant + green-bond. |

**Why #4:** Already a firm political commitment — highest political visibility action. Aligns with 3 C40 HIAs. Transport is the largest emissions sector in RS. Pairs with biomethane (Action 3) — biomethane can fuel transitional CNG buses before full electrification, or power grid charging stations.

### Action 5: Grid Resilience and Flood Protection (energy infrastructure)

**What:** Harden distribution network — underground critical segments, flood-proof substations, install smart switches, and protect pump stations that are critical for the R$6B drainage system.

**Cost-effectiveness:** Primarily an avoided-loss action. 30-60% reduction in SAIDI. Protects 1.49M residents from repeat outages.

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 30** — Sustainable urban drainage (SUDs) | Direct match for the energy co-benefit dimension. POA's R$6B drainage investment depends on reliable power for pump stations. Grid resilience is the energy sector's contribution to SUDs. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 31** — Emergency preparedness | Grid resilience is a foundational element of emergency response. Jan 2024 storm left 1.1M without power; May 2024 floods knocked out 138,000 connections + failed drainage pumps. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 29** — Cool surfaces to reduce urban heat | Indirect. A resilient grid supports cooling systems during heat waves. POA-RES-E-01 links urban canopy (cooling) to reduced grid stress. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 34** — Green spaces and urban canopy | Indirect. 20,000+ tree planting (PLAC Action 27) reduces cooling loads on the grid. NbS co-benefit that strengthens grid resilience. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-RES-E-01** — Energy resilience co-benefits | Cross-cutting resilience action. Explicitly links flood protection (PLAC Actions 21-22), heat wave response (Action 17), and urban canopy (Action 27) to energy infrastructure protection. |
| **PLAC** | **PLAC: R$6B drainage and flood protection** | City's largest single investment commitment. Grid resilience is a prerequisite for this investment to function — pump stations need power. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `grid-resilience-upgrade` | Proven maturity, 18-36 month timeline. Financing: dfi-concessional + green-bond. |

**Why #5:** Post-disaster urgency makes this politically and financially viable now. DFI funders prioritize resilience in post-flood context. Without grid resilience, the R$6B drainage investment is at risk. Not ranked higher because it requires utility cooperation (CEEE Equatorial) and has indirect rather than direct emissions reduction.

### Action 6: Community Solar + Storage for Vulnerable Neighborhoods

**What:** Deploy 0.1-2 MWp solar PV with battery storage in flood-affected and energy-poor neighborhoods. Provides 4-12 hours backup during outages and reduces energy costs for low-income households.

**Cost-effectiveness:** 50-200 tCO2e/MWp/year avoided | 50-500 low-income households per installation

| Source | Alignment | Detail |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 12** — Grid decarbonization (building-scale) | Distributed solar adds clean capacity at neighborhood level. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 31** — Emergency preparedness | Battery backup provides resilient power for critical loads during outages — directly supports emergency response in vulnerable areas. |
| **C40/HIAP** | **HIA 34** — Green spaces and urban canopy | Can be co-located with community green spaces and NbS (rain gardens, canopy) for combined resilience. |
| **PLAC** | **POA-RES-E-01** — Energy resilience co-benefits | Addresses the resilience gap exposed by May 2024 floods (138,000 without power). Battery storage for drainage pump stations is a critical use case. |
| **PLAC** | **RS-E-03** — RS Renewable Energy Policy Package | State distributed generation framework (Lei 14.898/2016) enables community-scale solar. Net metering (MMGD) active. |
| **Energy Catalog** | `community-solar-resilience` | Emerging maturity tier, 12-24 month timeline. Financing: climate-fund-grant + ppp-light. |

**Why #6:** Strongest equity and just-transition action. Addresses energy poverty and post-disaster resilience simultaneously. Ranked lower due to emerging maturity tier and need for community governance model. Climate fund eligibility is high (GCF/GEF prioritize resilience + equity). Pairs with grid resilience (Action 5).

## Sequencing Recommendation

```
Phase 1 (0-12 months) — Quick wins + foundations
├── Action 2: Sustainable buildings program (R$6M, policy/incentive rollout)
├── Action 1: Solar on public buildings (expand existing PPP)
└── Action 5: Grid resilience assessment + utility engagement

Phase 2 (6-24 months) — Capital deployment
├── Action 3: Biogas scale-up (10 → 200 biodigesters)
├── Action 4: Electric bus procurement + charging infra
└── Action 5: Grid hardening construction begins

Phase 3 (12-36 months) — Emerging + scaling
├── Action 6: Community solar + storage pilot (2-3 neighborhoods)
├── Action 4: E-bus fleet scaling beyond 100
└── Action 1: Remaining schools (500 target)
```

**Key sequencing logic:**
- RS-E-03 (state solar policy) is already active — it's the enabling foundation for Actions 1, 2, and 6
- Action 2 deploys first because it's the cheapest (R$6M) with the highest leverage (R$9/tCO2e)
- Action 5 (grid resilience) requires utility negotiation, so assessment starts early but construction follows
- Action 6 (community solar) waits for Phase 3 because it requires community governance model development and is at emerging maturity
